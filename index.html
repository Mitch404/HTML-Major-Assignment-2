Overview

The Touhou Project is a long-running series of 2D bullet hell games made by Team Shanghai Alice, which, despite its name, is a solo project run by Jun’ya Ota, who goes by the pen-name ZUN. The western genre name of bullet hell comes from the Japanese name Danmaku, which has a literal translation of “bullet curtain”, meaning “barrage”.

The genre is known for its remarkably high difficulty, where even completing a game on normal difficulty is no small effort. In traditional shooters, projectiles are aimed at the player and are fast but infrequent. Danmaku instead has slower projectiles with significantly higher volume, often aimed indiscriminately, or are aimed to purposefully “zone” a player and keep them contained within a small portion of the screen.

Below is a clip of the last boss in Touhou 11: Subterranean Animism.


Patterns can often be grouped into four different types, examples of which can be found in the Gallery. There are aimed shots, which force the player to consistently move in one direction; Spiral patterns, which can be used to zone a player or provide a pattern of projectiles to dodge; Zoning patterns, which deliberately force the player into constricted spaces to limit their room for dodging additional patterns; and lastly, randomized or pattern-based projectile layouts, which have an emphasis on making the player interpret the incoming layout of projectiles and dodge on the fly.
